Latiff Mohidin: Pago Pago (1960-1969)


Recounts the formative era of painter practice in the 1960s in conjunction with his adventures across Europe and Southeast Asia. The phrase "Pago Pago" in the title was formulated by the painter himself to create awareness created from this journey. As a way of thinking and working aimed at challenging the dominance of Western modernism at the time, Pago Pago led Latiff to review the syncretic approach to form in his work, and to dialogue with avant-garde thinkers in Southeast Asia. His first major exhibition in Europe, Latiff Mohidin: Pago Pago (1960â€“1969) offered more than 70 paintings, sketches and art prints, supported by archival documents, as well as placing him in a dialogue of equivalent European painters. Pago Pago Latiff Mohidin series contains sketches, paintings, sculptures, art prints and poems. Sketches of historical or natural phenomena, from Angkor temples to bamboo shoots and barns of various shapes, offer an understanding of Latiff Mohidin's cosmic perspective. As we move attention from canvas to paper to ink, every form of Pago Pago emerges from the earth as if it were a biomorphic object. In some cases, these incarnations are mixed together, showing how all of their shapes have interdependent elements.
